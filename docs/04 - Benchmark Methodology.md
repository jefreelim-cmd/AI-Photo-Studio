\# Benchmark Methodology



Every workflow must be benchmarked before adoption.



\---



\## Benchmark Images



A standard collection of photographs will be maintained.



These images will represent different restoration challenges including:



\- faded photographs

\- low-resolution images

\- blurry portraits

\- noisy photographs

\- heavily damaged photographs



The same benchmark images must be used for every workflow comparison.



\---



\## Evaluation Criteria



Each workflow will be assessed using:



\- identity preservation

\- facial restoration

\- overall image quality

\- detail recovery

\- sharpness

\- noise reduction

\- processing time

\- VRAM usage

\- ease of use



\---



\## Adoption



A workflow is only adopted if it provides a measurable improvement over the current standard.



\---



\## Human Review



Benchmark scores assist decision making.



Final approval always remains a human decision.
